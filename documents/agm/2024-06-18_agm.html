63rd Annual General Meeting 18 June 2024

Chair/Secretary’s Report (key points):

  • Thanks to all officers of EKBA and all clubs! Good competitions for Men and Women and congratulations to the winners!
  • At the start of the 2023/2024 season we held a successful referee course to which we now have some freshly qualified officials; special thanks to those who helped in the training.
  • Superb Finals Day & Awards congratulations and thanks to the organisers and to Saints for providing the table officials.
  • 2023/2024 saw a successful EKYBL (youth league) - Congratulationss to the organisers for this great development!
  • More trained officials are needed, therefore another referee course shall be held, and we would like volunteers.
  • Pre-season 3v3 would also benefit the league in the off season and the EKBA should garner interest again pre next season.

Minutes from end of season 2023/2024:

Approved & finalised.

Treasurers Report:

Final accounts show a good profit over the season (very good considering paying for 3 Referees courses!) with the final balance for the year being extremely healthy. Also, EKBA now has an online account so payment monitoring should run a lot smoother.

Proposed Rule Changes:

  • A nominated active & qualified referee to referee 10 games per season (up from 7) agreed and if necessary, the 10 can be made up by others from the same club. Entries to be made by 1st September. Via forms on Website.
  • Executive Committee to discuss further fees and invoices for BACS payments.
  • Teams must provide EKBA with details of all players licenses before competitions begin. preferably with ‘head shots’ and head shots should ideally be available on a team sheet at each game!
  • Individual Affiliations for men’s competitions: Discussed regarding national league players with clubs outside EKBA playing in the EKBL. Agreed that clubs should be allowed a maximum of 3 senior national league players in their team.
  • Players eligibility for Men’s Cup competitions was discussed: Agreed that for a player to be able to play in the Cup they will have had to play 3 games in the league before Dec 31st. Allowances may be considered by Executive Committee if a player had been unable to play due to injury/illness.
  • League Formation Men’s: Proposed that we should run 2 Men’s Leagues which would allow fairer competition and allow for development of young players and give all players more court time. This would be dependent on sufficient entries and Executive Committee would make a final decision based on entries. Agreed unanimously. 2 Men’s Leagues for 2024/2025 intended. If two leagues, each league will have its own cup competitions run in the same way as Women’s Cup.
  • Any national league players could only play in division 1 & only in A team(s).
